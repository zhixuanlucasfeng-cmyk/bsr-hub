# BSR Hub Two-Minute Team Statement

**Lucas — 0:00–0:24**  
Many useful products and professional spaces are too expensive to buy. A student may want to play a PS5 for one weekend, while a new creator may need a camera or studio for only two hours. Buying everything wastes money and leaves valuable items unused. We built BSR Hub to make access more affordable.

**Anna — 0:24–0:46**  
BSR Hub is one local platform where anyone can rent an item, list their own item, buy or sell second-hand goods, and book spaces such as studios, print rooms, workshops, and small factories. Movable products can be picked up or delivered, while permanent workspaces are used on site.

**Yicheng — 0:46–1:08**  
Trust is our biggest design challenge. We use verified accounts, condition information, deposits, reviews, protected locations, and clear order states. BSR Hub holds the payment until the transaction is completed, and our database rules prevent two people from booking the same item at the same time.

**Lucian — 1:08–1:29**  
Our research compared rental, resale, workspace, and delivery platforms. We learned that clear photos, fair prices, local discovery, and simple protection are essential. Our pricing algorithm looks at the PS5 model, age, condition, missing functions, and cleanliness, or the size and features of a workspace.

**Nasia — 1:29–1:58**  
Our project supports UN Goal 8, Decent Work and Economic Growth, by helping owners and small businesses earn from resources they already have. It supports UN Goal 10, Reduced Inequalities, by giving lower-income users affordable access instead of requiring ownership. BSR Hub turns unused things into local opportunity: rent, reuse, and build together.
